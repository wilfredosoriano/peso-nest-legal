2 What PesoNest is — and isn't

PesoNest is a personal finance tracker. You enter your transactions, cards, and loans manually, and the app helps you see where your money is going.

It is not a bank. It's not connected to any financial institution. It doesn't pull live data from your accounts. The numbers you see are only as accurate as what you put in — so if you forget to log an expense, PesoNest won't know either.

Think of it like a digital notebook for your finances. I built the notebook — you fill it in.

3 Your data, your responsibility

I can't verify whether what you enter is accurate, and I don't try to. If you log ₱5,000 when you spent ₱50,000, PesoNest will show you ₱5,000 — it has no way to know otherwise.

A few things I'd ask you to keep in mind:

  • Double-check your entries, especially for loans and large transactions
  • Keep your phone secure — anyone who picks it up can open the app
  • If you use the Joint Loan feature, only share the QR code with someone you actually trust
  • Back up your data regularly using the export feature in Settings

4 This isn't financial advice

I'm a developer, not a financial advisor. PesoNest can show you how much you've been spending on food, or how many months until your loan is paid off — but it can't tell you whether you should take out that loan in the first place, or how to invest your savings.

For decisions that actually matter — big loans, investments, tax stuff — please talk to someone qualified. Use PesoNest to stay organized, not as the final word on your finances.

5 Premium

Some features — like exporting your full data backup or downloading a CSV of your transactions — require a Premium subscription. Here's how it works in practice:

  • You subscribe through the App Store or Google Play, not directly through me
  • It renews automatically until you cancel — you can do that anytime in your Apple or Google account settings
  • Refunds go through Apple or Google. I don't handle billing on my end.

Premium is a fair trade — you pay once a year, you get the full app. I keep the lights on.

6 Joint Loans

This feature lets two people link the same loan record so both phones stay in sync. I'm proud of how it works, but there are a few things worth knowing before you use it.

When you generate a QR code, PesoNest stores the loan data on a server temporarily. Once the other person scans it, you're linked — they'll see updates when you make a payment, and vice versa. There's no way to unlink after someone claims the code, so be sure about who you're sharing it with.

The QR code is single-use. After it's scanned, it's done.

7 Device Transfer

Getting a new phone? Device Transfer is there for that. It generates a 6-character code that holds your data for 24 hours on a server, encrypted. Your new phone enters the code, pulls the data, and the server copy is deleted.

Don't share that code with anyone during the window — whoever enters it first gets the data.

9 If something goes wrong

I put real effort into making PesoNest reliable, but I'm one person and phones are unpredictable. If your device gets wiped, the app crashes, or something unexpected happens — I can't recover data that only existed on your phone.

Please export a backup from time to time. Settings → Export Data → JSON. It takes less than a minute and could save you from losing months of records.

To be clear: I'm not liable for data loss, or for any financial decisions you make using the app. PesoNest is a tool — how you use it is up to you.

10 Deleting the app

If you uninstall PesoNest, everything stored locally goes with it. There's no cloud backup I can restore for you. Export your data first if you think you might want it later.
